Welcome to Road Trippin'!
The goal of this project was to take a deep dive into the data on our nation's highway infrastructure. US AND GLOBAL INVESTMENT:

US Road/Highway Analysis
In 2017, the US invested 0.7% of its gross domestic product (GDP) into road/highway infrastructure, which amounted to around $182.58 per capita spending. While US GDP grew over the years, investment in road/highway infrastructure consistently declined. In 2009, US monetary spending on infrastructure reached a peak high, but in the last 10 years it has also been on a decline. This could have a direct correlation with road/highway "Condition". Since 2009, the percentage of road/highways in the US that are considered "Good Condition" has dropped almost 4%.
World Relation
Though the US is in the top 25% of the world in resilient infrastructure systems, it still falls behind other developed countries. Other developed countries spend almost twice as much of their GDP on infrastructure as the US. In 2018, The American Society of Civil Engineers rated America's infrastructure system as a D+ and recommeneded that spending increase by $2T over the next decade. America's infrastructure was built with standards of 50 years ago, but that obviously has not been able to keep up with the amount of natural disasters and population growth that has happened in the same time period. Investing more into infrastructure would also help America's economy in general, as about 11% of the US work force is currently employed in an infrastructure related occupation. The World Economic Forum’s Global Competitiveness Report ranked US as 10th in infrastructure quality in 2017, whereas in 2002 the US ranked 5th.
